# Bosun v0.8 — Roadmap

**Baseline:** v0.7 (polish round complete: launch UX, predictor accuracy, pre-flight robustness, state+rescue resilience, plus a follow-up wave of bug-hunt fixes — proc detection via cmdline, MCP goroutine leak, rescue salvage error-surfacing, cleanup/merge silent error fixes, phantom + lockfile refactors, fuzz + stress test suite).
**Anchor:** **Public launch readiness.** Every ask in this round exists to close the remaining gates in `CLAUDE.md`'s release stance: robust kickoff, working preflight, brief-authoring that lets a stranger succeed, and the GitHub-visibility flip itself.
**Status:** Planning. Drafted after the v0.7 polish round + bug-hunt landed and pushed to private origin (2026-05-17).

---

## Position relative to the public launch

This is the launch round. `CLAUDE.md` names three gates explicitly:

> robust kickoff (no silent init hangs), a working `bosun doctor`-style preflight, and a brief-authoring assistant that lets a new user succeed without writing 6 disjoint briefs from scratch.

Where we stand against those gates:

1. **Robust kickoff** — addressed by v0.6.2 (centralized git timeout) and v0.7 lane 3 (stale-branch refusal, merge pre-flight, `--force` progress). The init path no longer hangs silently.
2. **`bosun doctor` preflight** — **not yet implemented.** v0.5 introduced a 1-min load check; v0.7 polished it. Neither covers iCloud/Spotlight environmental hazards, orphan worktrees, permission gaps, or any of the other recurring "operator pain" findings the dogfood loop kept surfacing.
3. **Brief-authoring assistant** — `bosun suggest` exists from v0.5. It's never been validated by a non-maintainer; the new-user experience is unconfirmed.

Plus the gating activity: **external-repo trial #2** against a real codebase, never successfully completed on a healthy machine. STATUS.md from the pre-reboot snapshot explicitly named this as the gating step for the public launch flip.

**v0.8 closes those four items and then flips the switch.** No new feature surface beyond what the gates require.

---

## Why launch readiness, why now

Three observations driving the urgency:

1. **The polish floor has been reached.** v0.6 anchored on resilience; v0.7 anchored on operator friction. The bug-hunt waves after each round produced steadily smaller fixes — proc detection, claims phantom filter, cleanup silent errors, salvage error surfacing. The next compounding step isn't another internal round; it's putting bosun in front of strangers and learning what we can't see from inside.
2. **The safety contract is empirically validated.** Every dogfood round and the truncated trial #1 found bugs, but main was never touched without explicit merge invocation. The contract held under load, under SIGBUS, under operator-killed inits, under corrupted-gitdir crashes. The v0.7 lane 4 work even shipped explicit gitdir-corruption recovery. This is the trust signal a public README can credibly make.
3. **Holding longer compounds the wrong way.** Every extra in-house round drifts the codebase further from a fresh-eyes audit. Real-world contact is what surfaces the next set of priorities.

## The v0.8 asks

Six asks. The numbered ordering is by gate-criticality (highest first); the lane split lower down groups them for parallel execution.

### 1. `bosun doctor` — system preflight

A new `bosun doctor` subcommand that runs every environmental check we've manually performed during the dogfood loop and reports anything that would derail a new user. Specifically:

- **macOS File Provider detection.** Check whether `~/Documents/`, `~/Desktop/`, or any ancestor of the repo is iCloud-managed. Surface as a high-severity warning: phantom-file duplication will plague the operator. (The single biggest "right in front of us" hazard we've hit, repeatedly.)
- **Orphan worktree directories.** Walk the repo's parent looking for `<repo>-bosun-*` dirs that aren't registered with `git worktree list`. Report each one and offer `bosun doctor --fix` to safely relocate them.
- **Stale `.bosun/init.lock` + claims/state phantom files.** Wired into the existing phantom-detection helpers. Surface even though they're auto-handled — operators benefit from visibility into "you have N phantoms, the filter is suppressing them, here's the root-cause fix" (drop `.metadata_never_index`).
- **Git version + executable accessibility.** Refuse if git is < some minimum (2.40? whatever supports the worktree commands we depend on).
- **Write permissions on `.bosun/` and repo root.**
- **MCP daemon startup capability.** Spawn a tiny test daemon, verify socket bind succeeds, kill it. Catches the macOS-socket-path-length issue we worked around for tests.
- **Config file syntax.** Already covered by `bosun config validate` from v0.5; doctor delegates and reports.
- **Phantom branch refs under `.git/refs/heads/bosun/`.** Already detected by `--clean-phantoms` in init; doctor scans proactively.

**Why first:** every other ask depends on the operator's environment being correctly diagnosed. A new user landing on HN, cloning, and hitting Spotlight phantoms on the second `bosun init` is exactly the bad-experience scenario `CLAUDE.md` names.

### 2. `bosun suggest` polish + guided brief authoring

The existing `bosun suggest` produces a single brief from a high-level description, but the new-user flow is unclear:

- Does the operator know to run it? (Discoverable in `--help` but not surfaced from `bosun init`.)
- Does the produced brief actually work end-to-end on a real repo? (Never empirically verified by someone other than the maintainer.)
- Is there a guided mode for operators who don't know what a "lane" should contain?

**v0.8 piece:**

- Audit the existing `bosun suggest` against the gate ("a new user succeeds without writing 6 disjoint briefs from scratch"). Capture any rough edges in `docs/v0.8-suggest-audit.md`; fix the small ones inline.
- Add `bosun init --suggest "<description>"` — calls suggest under the hood and writes the brief, skipping the manual "suggest, save, init" three-step. Reduces the new-user onboarding to one command.
- (Stretch, only if it falls out cleanly) `bosun suggest --guided` walks the operator through "what are the lanes, what files does each touch, what depends on what" via prompts. Defer if it bloats scope.

**Why second:** suggest is the only surface that turns a stranger's vague "I want bosun to do X" into the structured brief bosun consumes. If it doesn't work for newcomers, the whole tool's discoverability is broken.

### 3. External-repo trial #2 — execute, document, gate

This is not code; it's an execution step that the launch flip is conditional on. Per `docs/v0.6-trial-protocol.md`:

1. Pick a clean external repo (the existing target is `~/Documents/Homelab/homelab-status-mcp`; honor the resume target STATUS.md preserved if it's still valid).
2. Run the full protocol — init, parallel sessions, merge, cleanup. Watch for safety-contract violations.
3. Write `docs/v0.7.x-trial-findings.md` documenting outcome (success → cleared gate; surfaced bugs → v0.8.1 follow-up before launch).

**Why third (despite being the launch gate):** doctor + suggest land first so the trial runs against the launch-ready binary. A trial against pre-doctor bosun re-discovers issues doctor would have caught.

### 4. CI via GitHub Actions

The repo is private with local-only `make check`. Going public means accepting PRs from people who don't have the maintainer's local environment. Without CI, the first community PR will be approved on faith.

**v0.8 piece:**

- `.github/workflows/check.yml` — runs `make check` on every PR. Matrix: macOS-latest + ubuntu-latest at minimum. (Windows can wait — many of our tests `t.Skip` on Windows and the fix is bigger than CI scope.)
- `.github/workflows/fuzz.yml` — scheduled weekly fuzz run with `FUZZTIME=10m make fuzz`. Failures upload the new corpus entries as artifacts so the maintainer can investigate.
- `.github/workflows/stress.yml` — scheduled `make stress` on macOS + ubuntu. Same artifact-upload pattern on failure.

**Why fourth:** doesn't gate the launch itself, but lands as the launch happens so the first PR gets the right reception.

### 5. Public-facing docs polish

The README, examples, and any user-facing docs need an audit through fresh eyes. Specifically:

- **README.md** — quick-start that gets someone from `brew install bosun` (or equivalent) to first parallel session in under 5 minutes. Current README likely assumes maintainer-context.
- **Architecture diagram.** A simple ASCII or mermaid diagram showing repo → worktrees → claude agents → MCP daemon → state files. The mental model is the load-bearing prereq for trusting the tool.
- **Safety contract section.** Already drafted in `docs/v0.6-roadmap.md` and probably surfaced in README via v0.6 round-1 session-4. Verify it's prominent.
- **Troubleshooting section.** Lift the recurring issues from `STATUS.md` and the trial findings: iCloud/Spotlight phantoms, orphan worktrees, "what to do when init hangs," "what to do when an agent crashes."
- **Demo GIF or video.** Optional but high-impact for HN. Defer if it slows the launch.

### 6. Licensing + release notes + tag

- Confirm `LICENSE` is present and is Apache 2.0 (per `CLAUDE.md`'s release stance).
- Catch `RELEASES.md` up from v0.2.0 → v0.7 → v0.8. Brief per-version blurbs sufficient.
- Tag `v0.7.0` retroactively at the head of v0.7's bug-hunt + refactor + tests landing (i.e., now).
- Tag `v0.8.0` at the end of this round.
- Decide on Homebrew formula vs. plain `go install` for the initial launch. Defer to operator preference.

## What's explicitly NOT in v0.8

Holding the line on scope per the compounding-not-commits principle:

- **TUI feature expansion.** No interactive merge resolution, no in-TUI claim editing, no fancier visualizations. The current TUI is sufficient for v0.1; richer UX is a v0.9+ story driven by real-user feedback.
- **Net-new MCP tools.** Doctor will need some MCP wiring for the in-flight session checks, but no new agent-facing tools beyond what the gate requires.
- **Hooks system expansion.** No new hook events, no extended hook scripting. v0.6's hook timeout enforcement is the floor; we don't move it.
- **Performance work.** Bosun is fast enough. Profiling is for the v1.x+ era.
- **Multi-machine / distributed sessions.** Single-machine remains the operating assumption. The v0.6 roadmap deferred this to "v1.0+ research project"; that still holds.
- **Windows test parity.** Many tests `t.Skip` on Windows. Real Windows support is real work and a poor fit for the launch sprint. Document the gap; defer to v0.9+.
- **Self-experimentation hazard** (from v0.7 deferred list) — only bites when bosun is working on bosun. Document in CLAUDE.md as a known gotcha; no code fix this round.
- **`scenarios_test.go` per-lane split refactor** (from v0.7 deferred list) — the convention is established (v0.7 lanes used `scenarios_<lane>_test.go`); no migration needed.

## Sequence

Four-lane round, lanes ordered by interdependence:

1. **Lane 1 — `bosun doctor` (ask #1).** New `cmd/bosun/cmd_doctor.go`, new `internal/doctor/` package with one health-check function per concern. Self-contained; pulls in `internal/phantom` and `internal/preflight` but doesn't modify them. Largest lane.

2. **Lane 2 — `bosun suggest` polish + `init --suggest` (ask #2).** Touches `cmd/bosun/cmd_suggest.go` and `cmd/bosun/cmd_init.go`. Audit doc lands in `docs/`. Stretch (guided mode) capped at "ship if clean."

3. **Lane 3 — CI + public-facing docs + licensing (asks #4, #5, #6).** No Go code; all `.github/workflows/`, `README.md`, `RELEASES.md`, `LICENSE`. Independent of lanes 1 + 2 by file.

4. **Lane 4 — Trial #2 execution + public-launch checklist (ask #3 + the flip itself).** Cannot start until lanes 1 + 3 land (doctor needs to exist; docs need to be ready). This lane is mostly operator-driven — running the trial protocol, writing findings doc, executing the actual `gh repo edit --visibility public` and `git tag v0.8.0 && git push --tags origin`.

## After v0.8

**The repo flips public.** That's the milestone. Specifically:

- `gh repo edit --visibility public`
- `git push origin v0.7.0 v0.8.0`
- Update `CLAUDE.md` to remove the "NOT public yet" release-stance section (or rewrite it to reflect the launched state).
- Optional: HN "Show HN: bosun" post, blog draft from `docs/blog/`.

Then **stop and listen for a week.** Watch issues, watch PRs, watch any community engagement. v0.9 priorities come from what real users actually hit, not from another internal round.

Resist the urge to keep building during that week. The compounding-not-commits principle is at its strongest right after a launch: every speculative feature you ship before the feedback lands trades against the feedback's signal.

## Open questions

1. **Doctor's `--fix` mode.** Some checks have an obvious autofix (drop `.metadata_never_index`, prune stale init.lock, rename orphan dirs to `_orphan-*`). Should the autofix path be opt-in (`bosun doctor --fix`) or proactive (auto-fix on every doctor run with a `--dry-run` to preview)? Bias toward opt-in for the launch — surprises during a preflight are bad UX.

2. **`init --suggest` UX shape.** Does it stream the LLM response inline, or generate the brief to a tmp file first and prompt the operator to review before init? The latter is safer (no surprise filesystem mutation) but more friction. Lane 2 operator's call.

3. **Trial #2 repo target.** STATUS.md preserved `~/Documents/Homelab/homelab-status-mcp` as the resume target. If that repo has drifted (the maintainer has been working on it independently), pick a different external repo OR reset homelab-status-mcp to its known-good SHA. Decide before lane 4 starts.

4. **Tagging strategy retroactively.** v0.6.2 was the last tagged version. The work between then and v0.8 landed without intermediate tags. Should we tag v0.7.0 at the v0.7 round-1 merge commit, then v0.7.1, v0.7.2, etc. for the bug-hunt waves? Or just tag v0.7.0 at the latest pre-v0.8 commit and skip the granularity? Bias toward "one tag covers v0.7 + its bug-hunt; v0.7.0 = a45793f-ish."

5. **Homebrew formula vs. `go install`.** Both work. Homebrew gives a cleaner first-run experience (one command, no Go toolchain assumption). `go install` keeps the maintenance surface tiny. Decide before lane 4 publishes.
